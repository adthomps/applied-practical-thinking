## Introduction

Prompt design is the art and science of crafting instructions that guide AI agents to produce useful, reliable, and safe outputs. This guide covers:

- The anatomy of a good prompt
- Common pitfalls
- Examples and anti-patterns

## Anatomy of a Good Prompt

- **Clear intent**: State what you want, not just what you don't want.
- **Context**: Provide relevant background or constraints.
- **Format**: Specify output structure if needed.
- **Examples**: Show what good/bad outputs look like.

## Common Pitfalls

- Vague instructions
- Overly broad or narrow scope
- Ignoring edge cases
- Failing to specify output format

## Examples

### Good Prompt

> "Summarize the following article in 3 bullet points. Focus on key findings."

### Bad Prompt

> "Summarize this."

## Anti-Patterns

- Asking for "anything you can tell me"
- Using ambiguous terms ("interesting", "important")
- Not specifying length or detail

## Conclusion

Prompt design is iterative. Test, refine, and document what works for your use case.
